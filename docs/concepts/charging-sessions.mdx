title: "Tracking your EV charging sessions"
description: "See how ChargeGo records every charge event, calculates energy consumed from battery levels, and computes your total cost at the end of a session."
---

A charging session is the record of energy actually delivered to your car during a reservation window. Where a reservation is a booking, a session is the evidence — it captures when charging started and ended, how much battery percentage was gained, how many kWh were consumed, and what you owe. Every session is linked to exactly one reservation.

## Session fields

| Field | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `id` | integer | Unique identifier. |
| `reservationId` | integer | The reservation this session belongs to. |
| `chargerId` | integer | The charger that delivered the energy. |
| `startBatteryLevel` | number (%) | Battery state of charge when the session began (0–100). |
| `endBatteryLevel` | number (%) | Battery state of charge when the session ended (0–100). |
| `chargedPercentage` | number (%) | Difference: `endBatteryLevel − startBatteryLevel`. |
| `startEnergyKwh` | number | Absolute energy in the battery at session start. |
| `endEnergyKwh` | number | Absolute energy in the battery at session end. |
| `consumedKwh` | number | kWh delivered during the session. |
| `unitPricePerKwh` | number | Price per kWh charged by the charger. |
| `totalCost` | number | Final cost: `consumedKwh × unitPricePerKwh`. |
| `startedAt` | string (ISO 8601) | Timestamp when charging began. |
| `endedAt` | string (ISO 8601) | Timestamp when charging ended. |
| `reservationStartTime` | string (ISO 8601) | The reservation's original start time. |
| `reservationEndTime` | string (ISO 8601) | The reservation's original end time. |
| `status` | enum | `ACTIVE` or `COMPLETED`. |

## How costs are calculated

ChargeGo calculates consumed energy and cost using only two inputs: your battery levels and your vehicle's `batteryCapacity` (stored in your vehicle profile).

```text
consumedKwh = (endBatteryLevel% − startBatteryLevel%) × batteryCapacity ÷ 100
totalCost   = consumedKwh × charger.pricePerKwh
```

**Example:** vehicle battery capacity is 75 kWh, session starts at 20% and ends at 80%, charger price is $0.40/kWh.

```text
consumedKwh = (80 − 20) × 75 ÷ 100 = 45 kWh
totalCost   = 45 × 0.40 = $18.00
```

Both values are rounded to two decimal places.

<Note>
The absolute energy fields `startEnergyKwh` and `endEnergyKwh` are derived from `batteryCapacity × batteryLevel% ÷ 100` and are provided for convenience. The billing calculation uses `consumedKwh`, which is computed from the difference in battery percentages.
</Note>

## Session statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `ACTIVE` | The session is in progress. Charging is happening now. |
| `COMPLETED` | The session has ended. `consumedKwh` and `totalCost` are final. |

## Starting a session manually

You can start a session yourself as soon as you are within the reservation window (i.e., between `startTime` and `endTime`). Attempting to start before `startTime` or after `endTime` will return a `409 Conflict`.

```http request
POST /sessions/start?reservationId=99&startBatteryLevel=20
```

`startBatteryLevel` is a percentage from 0 to 100. If you omit it, ChargeGo uses the `endBatteryLevel` from your most recent previous session for the same vehicle. If no previous session exists, it defaults to 20%.

<Tip>
Always provide an explicit `startBatteryLevel` if you want an accurate cost calculation. The default fallback value is a best-effort estimate and may not reflect your car's actual state of charge.
</Tip>

## Ending a session manually

When you unplug, send `POST /sessions/{id}/end` with your current battery level:

```http request
POST /sessions/42/end?endBatteryLevel=80
```

`endBatteryLevel` must be between 0 and 100, and must not be lower than `startBatteryLevel`. On success, the session moves to `COMPLETED`, `consumedKwh` and `totalCost` are calculated, and the charger status returns to `AVAILABLE`.

## Manual session flow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Arrive at the charger">
    Navigate to the station and find the charger matching your reservation's `chargerId`. Your reservation must be within its active time window.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start the session">
    Call `POST /sessions/start?reservationId={id}&startBatteryLevel={level}` with your current battery percentage. The session status becomes `ACTIVE` and the charger status changes to `OCCUPIED`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Plug in and charge">
    Connect your car. The session records `startedAt` at the moment you called the start endpoint.
  </Step>
  <Step title="End the session">
    When you're done, call `POST /sessions/{id}/end?endBatteryLevel={level}` with your final battery percentage. ChargeGo computes `consumedKwh` and `totalCost` and returns the completed session record.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Auto-start and auto-complete

You don't have to interact with the session endpoints manually. ChargeGo's scheduler handles sessions automatically:

- **Auto-start**: every 30 seconds the system checks for `ACTIVE` reservations whose `startTime` has arrived. If no session exists yet, one is created automatically using either the end battery level from the vehicle's last session or the configured default of 20%.
- **Auto-complete**: every 60 seconds the system checks for `ACTIVE` sessions whose reservation's `endTime` has passed. Those sessions are completed automatically. The system estimates `endBatteryLevel` based on actual charging duration and the charger's rated `powerOutput`, capped at 100%.

<Warning>
Auto-completed sessions estimate `endBatteryLevel` from time elapsed and charger power. For accurate billing records, end your session manually before `endTime` whenever possible.
</Warning>

## Viewing your session history

Use `GET /sessions` to list all charging sessions for your account. Use `GET /sessions/{id}` to retrieve a single session record. Sessions are scoped to your account — you will only see sessions linked to your own vehicles.
