Before you can book a charging slot, ChargeGo needs to know about your electric vehicle. A vehicle profile stores the details that the system uses to match your car to a compatible charger — most importantly, its connector type. Without at least one registered vehicle, the reservation flow will not let you proceed.

## Vehicle fields

Each vehicle record contains the following fields:

| Field | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `id` | integer | Unique identifier assigned by ChargeGo. |
| `brand` | string | Manufacturer name, e.g. `Tesla`. |
| `model` | string | Model name, e.g. `Model Y`. |
| `batteryCapacity` | number (kWh) | Total usable battery capacity. Used to calculate energy consumed during a session. |
| `connectorType` | enum | The physical connector standard your car uses. Must be one of `CCS`, `TYPE2`, or `CHADEMO`. |
| `plateNumber` | string | Vehicle licence plate. Must be unique across all ChargeGo accounts. |

<Note>
`plateNumber` is enforced as globally unique. If you try to register a plate already in the system, the request will be rejected with a `409 Conflict` error.
</Note>

## Connector types

ChargeGo supports three connector standards. Your vehicle's connector type must **exactly match** the charger's connector type when you make a reservation — the system enforces this automatically and will reject the booking if they differ.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="CCS (Combined Charging System)">
    CCS is a DC fast-charging standard widely adopted by European and American manufacturers, including vehicles from BMW, Ford, Hyundai, and Volkswagen. CCS chargers in ChargeGo can deliver up to 150 kW.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="TYPE2 (IEC 62196 Type 2)">
    TYPE2 is the dominant AC charging standard in Europe. It supports single- and three-phase AC charging and is the default connector on most new EVs sold in the EU. ChargeGo TYPE2 chargers operate at 22 kW AC.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="CHAdeMO">
    CHAdeMO is a DC fast-charging standard originating in Japan, used by Nissan, Mitsubishi, and some older Kia models. ChargeGo CHAdeMO chargers support up to 50 kW.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Warning>
Setting the wrong connector type on your vehicle profile will cause every reservation attempt to fail with a compatibility error. Double-check your car's manual or the charging port before registering.
</Warning>

```json vehicle response
{
  "id": 42,
  "brand": "Tesla",
  "model": "Model Y",
  "batteryCapacity": 75.0,
  "connectorType": "CCS",
  "plateNumber": "34ABC123"
}
```

## Managing your vehicles

<Steps>
  <Step title="Register a vehicle">
    Send a `POST /vehicles` request with your vehicle's details. The `brand`, `model`, `batteryCapacity`, `connectorType`, and `plateNumber` fields are all required.

    ```json request body
    {
      "brand": "Tesla",
      "model": "Model Y",
      "batteryCapacity": 75.0,
      "connectorType": "CCS",
      "plateNumber": "34ABC123"
    }
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="View your vehicles">
    Send a `GET /vehicles` request to list all vehicles registered to your account. Use `GET /vehicles/{id}` to fetch a single record.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update a vehicle">
    Send a `PUT /vehicles/{id}` with only the fields you want to change. All fields are optional in an update request.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Delete a vehicle">
    Send a `DELETE /vehicles/{id}`. ChargeGo will reject deletion if the vehicle has any reservation or charging session history attached to it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
You can register multiple vehicles under the same account — one for a personal car and one for a company fleet vehicle, for example. Each vehicle maintains its own reservation and session history.
</Tip>

## Why you must register a vehicle first

ChargeGo uses your vehicle's `connectorType` and `batteryCapacity` at two different points in the workflow:

- **At reservation time** — the system checks that your vehicle's `connectorType` matches the charger's `connectorType`. If they don't match, the reservation is blocked.
- **At session end** — the system multiplies `batteryCapacity` by the percentage charged to calculate the exact kWh consumed and your total cost.

This means a vehicle profile is a prerequisite for everything else in ChargeGo. Register your vehicle before searching for stations or making a reservation.
